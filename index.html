02 Manifesto

You're running someone else's code and calling it your life

Every opinion you hold — check it. Is it yours? Or did someone install it before you had the capacity to refuse? Most of what we call personality is just unexamined conditioning wearing a familiar face.

This isn't about fixing yourself. Nothing's broken. It's about peeling back all the shit that was layered on top of what was already working.

The body already knows. It's been screaming the answer while you've been Googling it. Bad sleep, chronic tension, inability to sit still, emotional reactivity — that's not a deficiency. That's signal. You just never learned to read it.

So we don't add. We remove. The goal is less — less noise, less input, less performance, less proving. What's left when you stop performing is the actual you. And that version doesn't need a system. It is the system.

Sleep is the foundation. Not a hack. Not 8 hours of discipline porn. The actual biological non-negotiable that every other function depends on. Get this wrong and nothing else matters. Training is structure — not punishment, not aesthetics, just honest load that your body can use. Food is fuel you actually need, not what some influencer ate on camera.

And then: wait. The hardest part. Especially if you're wired to move. The right move shows itself. Forcing the wrong one is how most people stay stuck while feeling productive.

No gurus. No followers. No community. Just individuals doing their own work. If you need someone to tell you what to do, you're not ready for this. When you are, you won't need to be told.

03 Library
001
The Definitive Book of Human Design Ra Uru Hu & Lynda Bunnell

The full system. Not the Instagram version, not the TikTok personality quiz. The actual mechanics of how you're designed to operate — decision-making, energy type, authority. Dense, but worth it if you're serious.

Key takeaways
  • You have a specific authority for making decisions — your mind is not it
  • Most of your "personality" is conditioning from other people's energy
  • Waiting isn't passive — for some types it's the entire strategy
002
Why We Sleep Matthew Walker

After reading this you won't set another 5am alarm. Walker lays out — with zero ambiguity — what sleep deprivation actually does to your brain, body, hormones, and lifespan. It's worse than you think.

Key takeaways
  • 6 hours of sleep is not enough. You're impaired and you can't tell
  • Alcohol doesn't help you sleep. It sedates you. Different thing
  • One bad night tanks your testosterone, immune function, and emotional regulation
003
The Body Keeps the Score Bessel van der Kolk

Your body stores everything you never processed. That tension, those reactions, the patterns you can't think your way out of — they're not in your head. They're in your tissues. This book explains why, and what to do about it.

Key takeaways
  • Trauma lives in the body, not in the story you tell about it
  • Talk therapy alone can't reach what's stored below the neck
  • The nervous system needs to feel safe before anything changes
004
Antifragile Nassim Nicholas Taleb

Resilience is not the goal. Resilience just means you survive the hit. Antifragile means you get stronger from it. Taleb rewires how you think about stress, volatility, and why comfort is the real risk.

Key takeaways
  • Small stressors build capacity. Removing all stress makes you fragile
  • The things that survive long-term are the things that benefit from disorder
  • Optionality beats prediction every time
005
Breath James Nestor

You've been breathing wrong your entire life. Through your mouth, into your chest, too fast, too shallow. Nestor spent years testing what happens when you fix it. Blood pressure drops, anxiety drops, everything changes.

Key takeaways
  • Mouth breathing at night wrecks your sleep and your face structure
  • Slow nasal breathing shifts your entire nervous system state
  • Most "anxiety" is just a breathing pattern you never corrected
006
Meditations Marcus Aurelius

Private journal of the most powerful man on earth, never meant to be published. No performance, no audience. Just a guy reminding himself every morning to not be a reactive idiot. 2,000 years old and still more useful than anything on your feed.

Key takeaways
  • You don't control what happens. You control what you do with it
  • Most of your suffering is your opinion about the situation, not the situation
  • Do the work. Skip the credit. Repeat until dead
007
The War of Art Steven Pressfield

Resistance is the thing between you and the work you know you should be doing. Pressfield names it, dissects it, and makes you feel like an idiot for ever listening to it. Short book. Hits hard.

Key takeaways
  • The more important the work, the more resistance you'll feel
  • Amateurs wait for inspiration. Professionals show up regardless
  • Resistance is impersonal — it doesn't care who you are. It just blocks
008
Letting Go David R. Hawkins

The mechanism is simple. Feel the feeling fully without labelling, analysing, or acting on it. Let it be there until it passes. It always passes. The whole book is basically that, said enough ways until it actually lands.

Key takeaways
  • Suppressed emotions don't disappear. They run your life from underneath
  • Surrendering isn't weakness. It's dropping the resistance that's exhausting you
  • You don't think your way to peace. You feel your way there
