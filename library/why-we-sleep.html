Why We Sleep

Matthew Walker

The Book

After this book you'll never set a 5am alarm again. Walker lays out what sleep deprivation does to every system in your body. Not opinion — peer-reviewed research. And it's way worse than you think.

The chapters on memory consolidation and emotional regulation alone are worth the read.

Key Takeaways

  • 6 hours is not enough. You're impaired and you can't tell
  • Alcohol doesn't help you sleep. It sedates you. Different thing entirely
  • One bad night tanks testosterone, immune function, and emotional regulation
  • Dreaming isn't random — it's your brain processing trauma and building creativity
  • The economic cost of sleep deprivation is bigger than smoking, alcohol, and physical inactivity combined
