Skin in the Game

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

The Book

Don't trust anyone who doesn't eat their own cooking. Taleb on real risk, no-bullshit sovereignty, and why the people making decisions should bear the consequences. Most of the modern world is built on the opposite.

Angry, brilliant, repetitive in the best way. He keeps hammering the same nail because the nail keeps coming loose. If you've ever wondered why so many experts are wrong about everything, this book explains the structural reason.

Key Takeaways

  • If you see fraud and don't say fraud, you are a fraud
  • What's fragile should break early while it's still small
  • The IYI (Intellectual Yet Idiot) class is running the world and they have no skin in the game
  • Symmetry matters. Never take advice from someone who doesn't have to live with the consequences
  • Survival comes first. Everything else is noise until you're still standing
