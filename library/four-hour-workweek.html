The 4-Hour Workweek

Tim Ferriss

The Book

Half the tactics are dated. The philosophy is not. Ferriss reverse-engineers work, retirement, and what a life is supposed to look like. The point isn't four hours. The point is questioning every default.

Read it for the mindset, not the virtual assistant hacks. The idea that you can design your life instead of accepting the one handed to you. That was radical in 2007 and it's still radical now because most people still haven't done it.

Key Takeaways

  • Pareto's law: 80% of results come from 20% of effort
  • Batch tasks. Checking email twice a day is enough
  • Outsource everything that costs less than your time is worth
  • "Someday" is a disease that will take your dreams to the grave
  • Ask for forgiveness, not permission. Most rules are guidelines that nobody enforces
